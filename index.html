Profile

I am a machine learning researcher and engineer in the bay area. I currently work at the Feed Relevance team in LinkedIn. I developed several features into LinkedIn feed ranking models that significantly improved the user engagement on LinkedIn feeds. Some parts of my old work are summarized in the blog "Making Your Feed More Relevant": part1 part2.

I received my Ph.D. in School of Computing and Information Sciences in Florida International University under the supervision of Prof. Tao Li. In the Ph.D. study, I worked on the areas of recommender systems and event mining, and published more than 20 research papers on top conferences including ACM SIGKDD, CIKM, ICDM and RecSys. I also had 2 summer internships in IBM T.J. Watson Research Center (supervised by Larisa Shwartz) and 1 summer internship in LinkedIn.

My Curriculum Vitae is available upon request.

Research Interest

Broadly interested in data and information analysis including data mining, machine learning and information retrieval techniques. More specifically, I focus on user modeling and personalization in recommender systems, online advertising, multi-arm bandit algorithms, event mining that discovers and summarizes the hidden relations of the event data, streaming data, time series data and textual data.
